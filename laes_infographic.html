Cryogenic Energy Storage for Long-Duration Applications

Technology Overview

Liquid Air Energy Storage (LAES), also known as Cryogenic Energy Storage (CES), is a thermo-mechanical energy storage technology that stores energy by liquefying air. This technology addresses the need for long-duration energy storage solutions that can discharge for extended periods (4 to 12+ hours or multi-day), crucial for managing renewable energy intermittency.

Efficiency & Performance

Round-Trip Efficiency

Typically ranges from 50-60% but can be improved to potentially 70%+ by effectively integrating waste heat or cold recovery and storage systems.

The system can be enhanced by incorporating thermal storage and integrating with industrial processes to capture waste heat during liquefaction and use it during the power recovery phase, significantly improving overall efficiency.
