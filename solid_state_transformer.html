Problems Being Addressed

Facilitating the seamless connection of DC sources (like solar PV, batteries, EV chargers) and AC loads/grids operating at different voltage and frequency levels. Power quality issues: Mitigating voltage sags/swells, harmonics, and power factor issues in distribution networks.

How It Works

A Solid-State Transformer (SST), also known as a Power Electronic Transformer (PET), replaces the traditional copper-and-iron core transformer with a system based on power electronics converters and a high-frequency transformer.

Advanced control systems manage power electronic switches
(like SiC or GaN MOSFETs/IGBTs) for precise operation

Multi-Stage Conversion Process

1

AC to DC

Rectification of input AC power to DC

2

High-Frequency Isolation

High-frequency transformer provides isolation and voltage transformation

3

High-Frequency AC to DC

Rectification of high-frequency AC output

4

DC to AC Inversion

Conversion to final AC output (if AC output needed)
