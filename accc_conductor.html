Advanced Carbon-Core Transmission Technology

Challenge Addressed

Limited capacity of existing transmission corridors - many existing power lines are operating at their thermal limits, creating bottlenecks and restricting the integration of new generation, especially renewables. Acquiring right-of-way and constructing new transmission infrastructure is expensive and faces public opposition.

How It Works

Carbon-core cables, with ACCC (Aluminium Encapsulated Carbon-core Conductor) being a prime example, replace the traditional steel core of conventional ACSR (Aluminium Conductor Steel Reinforced) cables with a lightweight, high-strength carbon fibre composite core.

This composite core is significantly lighter and stronger than steel, and has a much lower coefficient of thermal expansion.
