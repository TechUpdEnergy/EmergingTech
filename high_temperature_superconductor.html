Zero-Resistance Power Transmission Technology

Challenge Addressed

High electrical losses in conventional conductors - resistive losses in copper and aluminium cables lead to significant energy waste. In dense urban areas, it's difficult to install enough conventional cables to meet growing demand due to space limitations. High fault currents in modern grids can damage equipment and cause instability.

How It Works

High-Temperature Superconductors (HTSC) are materials that exhibit zero electrical resistance and expel magnetic fields when cooled below a critical temperature. "High temperature" is relative to traditional low-temperature superconductors and typically means they can operate at temperatures achievable with liquid nitrogen.
