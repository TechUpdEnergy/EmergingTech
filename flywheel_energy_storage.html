Fast-Response Kinetic Energy Storage Systems

Challenge Addressed

Rapid and Short-Duration Frequency Deviations: Addressing very fast (sub-second to minutes) frequency fluctuations in the power grid that require extremely quick injection or absorption of power. Grid Stability with High IBR Penetration: Providing fast-acting inertial-like support (synthetic or fast inertia) and smoothing out very rapid power fluctuations from renewable sources.

Key Components

üåÄ Flywheel Rotor

A precisely balanced, heavy cylindrical mass, often made of steel or advanced composite materials like carbon fiber (for higher rotational speeds and energy density), that spins at very high velocities.

‚ö° Motor/Generator

An integrated electrical machine that acts as a motor during charging, using electricity from the grid to accelerate the flywheel. During discharge, it acts as a generator, converting kinetic energy back into electricity.

üîå Power Electronics Interface

Bi-directional power converters (AC/DC rectifiers and DC/AC inverters) manage electricity flow between the flywheel's motor/generator and the AC grid, enabling rapid response to grid signals.
