Challenge Addressed

Addressing the need for long-duration energy storage solutions that can discharge for extended periods (typically 4 to 12+ hours, or even multi-day), which is crucial for managing the intermittency of renewable energy sources over daily or longer cycles.

Operation Process

üîã Charging

Electricity drives electrochemical reactions in the cell stack, changing the oxidation state of electroactive species. The "charged" electrolytes are stored in tanks.

‚ö° Discharging

Charged electrolytes are pumped through the cell stack where reverse electrochemical reactions occur, releasing electricity back to the grid.

Common Chemistries

Vanadium Redox (VRFB)

Uses vanadium ions in different oxidation states. Most mature and widely deployed type.

Zinc-based

Utilizes zinc chemistry for cost-effective long-duration storage applications.

Iron-based

Employs abundant iron materials for sustainable energy storage solutions.

Organic Flow

Uses organic molecules for environmentally friendly storage systems.
