🔀 Modular SSSC (mSSSC)

Advanced Series FACTS Technology for Power Flow Control

Challenge Addressed

Relieving bottlenecks on existing AC transmission lines by actively managing power distribution among parallel paths. Limited grid capability in flexible power routing to accommodate fluctuating renewable energy sources.

Transmission Bottlenecks = Stranded Renewable Energy

Advanced Technology Foundation

🔧 Modular Multi-Level Converter (MMC) Based

An mSSSC is an advanced series Flexible AC Transmission System (FACTS) device based on Voltage Source Converter (VSC) technology, specifically utilizing a modular multi-level converter (MMC) topology similar to that used in MMC-HVDC systems. This provides superior performance, scalability, and reliability.

The system is connected in series with a transmission line and injects a controllable voltage in series with the line. The voltage phasor is controllable in both magnitude and phase angle, enabling precise power flow management.

How mSSSC Controls Power Flow

1

Series Connection

Connected in series with transmission line to directly influence power flow

2

3

4

Power Flow Management

Directly controls active power flowing through the line

🇬🇧 National Grid ESO (UK) - Stability Pathfinder Program

National Grid ESO has been at the forefront of mSSSC deployment as part of its innovative Stability Pathfinder projects. These strategic installations are specifically designed for managing power flows to unlock additional capacity on existing transmission lines, directly facilitating renewable energy integration. The projects implemented between 2022-2024 represent some of the world's most advanced applications of series FACTS technology, demonstrating how mSSSC can transform grid operations and enable higher renewable penetration without requiring new transmission infrastructure.

🇺🇸 Major US Utility Deployments

Leading American utilities including Xcel Energy, American Electric Power (AEP), and others have strategically deployed or extensively piloted mSSSC technology to address critical grid challenges. These implementations focus on alleviating transmission congestion, dramatically improving asset utilization, and providing sophisticated power flow management capabilities. AEP's deployment of mSSSC across its extensive network has been particularly notable for enhancing grid flexibility and enabling more efficient integration of renewable energy resources across multiple states.
