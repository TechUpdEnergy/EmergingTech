Advanced Automated Power System Protection

Challenge Addressed

Traditional protection schemes may not perform optimally with high DER penetration, bidirectional power flows, and dynamic network topologies. IED relays simplify site commissioning and enable rapid fault detection and isolation.

How IEDs Work

IEDs for protection (often called protective relays) are microprocessor-based devices that monitor electrical parameters (voltage, current, frequency, phase angle) in real-time.

Response Time: Within Milliseconds

Automated Protection Process

1

Sensing

Receive inputs from instrument transformers (CTs and VTs/PTs) that measure conditions on power lines, transformers, busbars, etc.

2

Logic Processing

Using embedded algorithms and settings (often based on IEC 61850), continuously analyze inputs to detect abnormal conditions according to predefined protection functions

3

Automated Decision

If a fault is detected, the IED's logic makes an autonomous decision to operate based on protection algorithms

4

Initiating Action

Sends a trip signal to relevant circuit breaker(s) to isolate the faulted section of the power system

5

Data Logging

Records detailed fault information (waveforms, event sequences) and communicates data to SCADA systems for analysis
