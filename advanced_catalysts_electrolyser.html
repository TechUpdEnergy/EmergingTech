Next-Generation Green Hydrogen Production

Challenge Addressed

Traditional electrolysers, especially PEMs, use expensive precious metal catalysts (like platinum and iridium), contributing significantly to the overall cost of green hydrogen. The water-splitting process is energy-intensive; improving the efficiency of electrolysers reduces the amount of renewable electricity needed per kg of hydrogen.

Electrolysers use electricity to split water into hydrogen and oxygen. Catalysts are used at the anode (for the Oxygen Evolution Reaction - OER) and cathode (for the Hydrogen Evolution Reaction - HER) to facilitate these reactions by lowering their activation energy.

üî¨ For PEM Electrolysers

Challenge: Typically require platinum for HER and iridium for OER due to acidic environment.

Solution: Developing ultra-low PGM loading catalysts, more durable PGM alloys, or exploring PGM-free catalysts (though challenging for OER in acidic PEMs).

‚ö° For AEM & SOEC Systems

Advantage: Can operate in alkaline (AEM) or high-temperature (SOEC) conditions.

Solution: Developing high-performance, durable catalysts based on earth-abundant transition metals (e.g., nickel, cobalt, iron oxides/hydroxides/phosphides).
