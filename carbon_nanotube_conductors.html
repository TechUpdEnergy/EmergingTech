Ultra-Lightweight High-Performance Transmission Cables

Challenge Addressed

Limited ampacity of existing conductors - conventional conductors like copper and aluminium have thermal limits that restrict current flow. High weight of conventional conductors requires strong, costly support structures (towers, poles) and limiting span lengths.

How It Works

CNTs are allotropes of carbon with a cylindrical nanostructure. Metallic CNTs can have electrical conductivity comparable to or even exceeding copper, but at a fraction of the weight.

For T&D applications, individual CNTs are processed and aligned to form macroscopic yarns, ribbons, or tapes. These can then be used as the core conductive element in a power cable, potentially combined with other materials (e.g., in a composite structure like CNT-aluminum or CNT-copper) to improve overall performance and manufacturability.

Their lightweight nature, high strength, and high current-carrying capacity could allow for more power transmission through existing corridors, reduced line sag, and lighter support structures.
