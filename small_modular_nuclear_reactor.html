How It Works

Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) are advanced nuclear reactors that use the heat generated from nuclear fission to produce steam, which drives a turbine connected to a generator to produce electricity for the T&D grid. Their design emphasizes modularity, factory fabrication, scalability, and enhanced safety features. Key components are manufactured in a factory environment and then transported to the site for assembly.
