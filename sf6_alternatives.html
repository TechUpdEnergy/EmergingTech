Challenge Addressed

Sulfur hexafluoride (SF6) is an excellent dielectric and arc-quenching medium used extensively in high and medium-voltage switchgear (like gas-insulated switchgear - GIS, circuit breakers). However, SF6 has a severe environmental impact.

How They Work

The electronegative nature of these fluorinated molecules helps to capture free electrons, preventing electrical breakdown (insulation). During an arc (e.g., when a circuit breaker opens), they help to cool and extinguish the arc rapidly.

These molecules are typically not used in pure form but are mixed with buffer gases like CO‚ÇÇ, O‚ÇÇ, or N‚ÇÇ to achieve the necessary performance across various voltage levels and temperatures.
