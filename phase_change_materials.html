Advanced Thermal Management for Power Equipment

Challenge Addressed

Transformers, power electronics, cables, and switchgear generate heat during operation. Excessive temperatures can lead to degradation of insulation, reduced efficiency, premature aging, and even catastrophic failure.

How PCMs Work

Phase Change Materials (PCMs) are substances that absorb or release large amounts of latent heat when they undergo a change in their physical state (e.g., from solid to liquid or liquid to gas, or solid-solid transitions) at a nearly constant temperature.

Heat Absorption (Melting)

When equipment temperature rises to the PCM's melting point, the PCM absorbs heat and melts, storing heat as latent heat of fusion. This prevents temperature from rising significantly further.

Heat Release (Solidifying)

When equipment cools down or ambient temperature drops below the PCM's solidification point, the PCM releases stored latent heat and solidifies, ready for the next heating cycle.
